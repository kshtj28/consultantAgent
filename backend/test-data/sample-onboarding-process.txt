# Customer Onboarding Process

## Overview

The customer onboarding process is critical for ensuring new customers can effectively use our services. This document outlines the current state and challenges.

## Current Process

### Stage 1: Initial Contact
- Sales team captures customer information in CRM
- Customer receives welcome email template
- Account manager assigned within 24-48 hours

### Stage 2: Documentation
- Customer completes:
  - Service agreement (paper-based)
  - Tax forms
  - Banking details for billing
- Documents sent via email, printed, signed, scanned, and returned

### Stage 3: Account Setup
- IT team creates user accounts manually
- Access permissions configured individually
- Training scheduled via Outlook calendar

### Stage 4: Go-Live
- Customer receives login credentials
- Initial training session (2 hours)
- 30-day support period begins

## Pain Points

1. **Document Handling**: Paper-based contracts cause delays
2. **Manual Account Setup**: IT involvement creates bottleneck
3. **Communication Gaps**: Multiple teams without unified tracking
4. **Training Scheduling**: Manual back-and-forth for availability
5. **No Self-Service**: Customers cannot track their own progress

## Automation Opportunities

| Task | Current | Automation Potential |
|------|---------|---------------------|
| Document Signing | Manual (3-5 days) | E-signature (same day) |
| Account Creation | Manual IT (1-2 days) | Automated provisioning |
| Training Booking | Email (1-3 days) | Self-service calendar |
| Status Updates | On-request | Automated notifications |

## Success Criteria

- Reduce time-to-value from 14 days to 3 days
- Improve customer satisfaction (NPS) by 20 points
- Reduce onboarding cost per customer by 40%
- Achieve 100% digital document completion

## Stakeholders

- **Sales Team**: Initiates onboarding
- **Account Management**: Oversees customer relationship
- **IT Department**: Manages technical setup
- **Training Team**: Provides customer education
- **Customer Success**: Ensures adoption and satisfaction
